Syllabus

  • Introduction to the concept of reliability.
  • Review of probability and statistics.
  • Reliability, hazard rate, cumulative hazard rate, load-strength interference, material degradation, time to failure.
  • System reliability analysis: reliability block diagrams, series system, parallel system, k out of n system, fault tree analysis.
  • Taylor's series-based approaches for reliability analysis: FOSM, FORM, SORM.
  • Simulation-based methods for reliability analysis: Monte Carlo simulation, importance sampling, latin hypercube sampling.
  • Reliability based design optimization and way ahead.

References

  • Haldar, A. & Mahadevan, S. (2000). Probability reliability and statistical methods in engineering design. John Wiley & Sons.
  • O'Connor, P.D.T. & Kleyner, A. (2011). Practical reliability engineering. John Wiley & Sons.
  • McPherson, J.W. (2019). Reliability physics and engineering: time-to-failure modeling. Springer.
