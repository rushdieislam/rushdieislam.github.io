Course content

Historical overview; Fatigue design philosophies; Stress-life approach: SN diagram, mean stress effects, notches; Strain-life approach: hysteresis loop, Bauschinger effect, shakedown, ratcheting, energy methods, Neuber's rule; Damage-tolerance approach: introduction to fracture, crack tip plasticity, fracture toughness, crack growth stages, crack closure, small fatigue cracks; Physics of fatigue: imperfections in crystalline solids, slip bands, intrusions/extrusions, crack initiation, microstructure effects; Residual stresses: origin, measurement methods, relationship with fatigue; Fatigue under variable amplitude loading: load spectrums, linear and nonlinear cumulative damage models, load interaction and sequence effects, rainflow method, life estimation; Multiaxial fatigue: multiaxial stress state, proportional and nonproportional loading, yielding and plasticity in multiaxial fatigue, stress-based criteria (Sines method), strain- and energy-based approaches, critical plane approach (Fatemi-Socie model); Environmental effects: corrosion, fretting, thermomechanical damage (creep and creep-fatigue interactions), neutron irradiation; Statistical aspects of fatigue: regression analysis of fatigue data, fatigue scatter, lognormal and Weibull distributions, tolerance limits, reliability analysis.

Literature review

Selecting a research paper

Choose a research paper from a reputed journal. Below is a list of suggested journals; however, you are free to select from other reputable journals as well:
  • International Journal of Fatigue
  • Acta Materialia
  • International Journal of Plasticity
  • Journal of the Mechanics and Physics of Solids
  • Proceedings of the Royal Society A
  • npj Computational Materials
The chosen paper should focus on a topic related to fatigue but must not be a review article.

Suggested topics

While you may select any topic related to fatigue, here are some suggestions for guidance:
  • Environmental effects on fatigue: Includes thermomechanical fatigue, corrosion, and irradiation effects.
  • Application of advanced experimental methods to study fatigue damage: Techniques such as high-resolution digital image correlation (HR-DIC), high-energy X-ray diffraction microscopy (HEDM), and diffraction contrast tomography (DCT).
  • Advanced computational methods for fatigue modeling: Includes crystal plasticity finite element (CPFE), molecular dynamics (MD), field dislocation mechanics (FDM), discrete dislocation dynamics (DDD), and discrete dislocation plasticity (DDP).
  • AI/ML in fatigue: Includes discovery of fatigue driving mechanisms, fatigue life prediction, reduced-order modeling, etc.

Key focus areas while reading the paper

  • Key contributions: What are the main findings and advancements presented in the paper?
  • Novelty: What makes the approach or findings unique?
  • Limitations: Are there any limitations in the research?
  • Future potential: What are the opportunities for extending or applying the work?

Presentation

You will deliver a 10 to 15-minute presentation on your selected paper. The presentation should focus on the key contributions, novelty, limitations, and potential future work of the paper.
