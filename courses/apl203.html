Syllabus

  • Part I: Newton-Euler Mechanics
    • Preliminaries: coordinate systems, vectors, tensors, tensor product; coordinate transformation.
    • Rotating frames; rotation tensor; Euler angles; angular velocity.
    • Rigid-body kinematics: five-term acceleration formula; examples.
    • Rigid-body kinetics: linear momentum; angular momentum; inertia tensor; kinetic energy.
    • Rigid-body kinetics: balance laws; governing equations; Euler's equations.
    • Examples: rigid body in free space; gyroscopes.
  • Part II: Analytical Mechanics
    • Generalized coordinates; constraints; degrees of freedom.
    • Principle of virtual work in statics: virtual displacements; virtual work; constraint forces; workless constraints; principle of virtual work; Lagrange multipliers; equilibria and stability of conservative systems; examples.
    • Dynamics: D'Alembert's principle; Lagrange's equations; Kane's equations; holonomic and nonholonomic systems; examples.
    • Conservative systems; Legendre transformation; Hamiltonian mechanics; energy theorem; examples.
  • Part III: Vibrations
    • Single degree of freedom system: free, damped, forced; examples.
    • Two-degree of freedom systems: normal modes; examples.
    • Extension to multi-degree of freedom systems; examples.
    • Introduction to nonlinear vibrations and stability analysis; examples.

Project (optional)

Brief description
  • The aim of the project is to study and simulate the spin reversal behavior shown by a rattleback.
  • You may watch this YouTube video or stop by my office for a demonstration of the spin reversal.
  • Read the "Spin reversal of the rattleback: theory and experiment" paper by Garcia and Hubbard. Derive equations of motion. Create a simulator in MATLAB and demonstrate the spin reversal behavior.
Deliverables and timeline
  • Submit a report along with MATLAB codes by 10th November 2023. There are three minimum requirements:
